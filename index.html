About Me

I am a data analyst and medical writer with over two years of experience in analysing secondary medical claims data and developing visualisations for medical studies. My work combines data analysis, technical writing, and client communication. I use R, SQL, Python, and Excel to prepare protocols, reports, and manuscripts in close collaboration with clients and interdisciplinary teams.

I hold a PhD in Evolutionary Anthropology from the Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Anthropology, where I investigated the role of climate change in Neanderthal extinction through speleothem-based palaeoclimate reconstructions. My academic background also includes a MSc in Archaeological Science, specialising in Archaeometry from the Australian National University, with a focus on U-Th dating methods. These research experiences provided me with a strong foundation in geochemical analysis, data interpretation, and scientific communication.

In my current role, I conduct statistical analyses using SQL Server and R, prepare visualisations and tables for reports, and proofread documents for clarity and consistency. I regularly present findings through Excel workbooks, PowerPoint presentations, and written summaries in both English and German. I have also contributed to the quality assurance of study outputs and supported the development of internal documentation standards.

Since entering the field, I have earned a certification in technical writing, gained proficiency in Microsoft SQL Server, and deepened my understanding of the regulatory context of secondary health data in Germany. I am currently expanding my knowledge in statistics and programming to strengthen my data science skillset.

Looking ahead, I aim to work on larger-scale health data projects and continue contributing to meaningful, data-driven research in collaboration with diverse teams.
